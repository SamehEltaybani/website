Biography

My teaching is shaped by a comprehensive, learner centered approach that integrates global awareness, practical experience, and interdisciplinary understanding. Drawing on experience across diverse cultural and educational contexts, I create inclusive learning environments that recognize and value the backgrounds and needs of all students. I bring a holistic perspective to healthcare education, enabling learners to understand not only their individual roles but also the broader systems in which they operate. My teaching experience spans a wide range of audiences, from undergraduate students to advanced researchers and community members, allowing me to adapt methods, pace, and content to suit different levels and needs. I emphasize interdisciplinary collaboration, reflecting the realities of healthcare practice, and prepare learners to work effectively across professional boundaries. My teaching is grounded in clinical experience and consistently oriented toward real world application, ensuring that knowledge is meaningful and relevant. As an active researcher, I integrate evidence based principles into my teaching, guiding learners in critical thinking and systematic problem solving. I adopt a flexible and adaptive approach that recognizes individual differences, providing tailored support to help each learner achieve their full potential. Clear communication is central to my practice, particularly in diverse and multilingual contexts, where I ensure that complex concepts are accessible without compromising accuracy. I also have extensive experience supporting learners who face challenges, using structured and empathetic approaches to build confidence and competence. My teaching is further strengthened by the ability to adapt to resource constrained environments, using creative strategies to deliver effective and impactful education under varying conditions.

Teaching Philosophy

I believe that every learner brings unique strengths, and that the role of a teacher is to create the conditions where those strengths can flourish. My philosophy is rooted in respect, evidence, and the conviction that healthcare education must always circle back to the patient and the family.

Mission

To equip current and future healthcare professionals with the knowledge, skills, and compassion they need to deliver evidence‑based, culturally sensitive care — using the best available resources and a deep understanding of each learner’s journey.

Strategy

My teaching strategy combines interactive, case‑based methods with structured mentorship. I adapt content to the learner’s level and context, integrating real‑world clinical experience and the latest research findings. Clear communication and continuous feedback are the pillars of my approach, ensuring that every session is both rigorous and inclusive.

A Global and Multicultural Perspective

My teaching is deeply informed by a sustained engagement with diverse cultural and educational environments. Having studied and worked in different countries, I have developed a practical understanding of what it means to learn and function as a minority within a foreign setting. This experience allows me to recognize challenges that are often unspoken, including language barriers, cultural expectations, and differences in academic systems. I integrate this awareness into my teaching by creating a learning environment that is inclusive, respectful, and accessible to all students regardless of their background. I actively work to remove invisible barriers between learners from different cultures by fostering mutual understanding and open communication. At the same time, my research engagement in international collaboration has provided me with a broad perspective on both global and local healthcare challenges. This enables me to teach in a way that connects international perspectives with local realities, ensuring that learners can relate their knowledge to both contexts. My approach therefore goes beyond cultural sensitivity and moves toward cultural integration, where diversity becomes a source of strength within the learning environment. Through this perspective, I support learners not only in acquiring knowledge, but also in developing the ability to engage effectively in diverse and evolving professional settings.

A Holistic Understanding of Healthcare

I approach teaching with a comprehensive understanding of healthcare as an interconnected system rather than a collection of isolated roles or tasks. My experience across education, clinical practice, research, management, and policy allows me to present learners with a complete and integrated view of healthcare. I emphasize that effective professional performance depends on understanding how individual actions contribute to broader system outcomes. In my teaching, I consistently highlight the relationships between different components of healthcare, including how decisions at one level influence outcomes at another. This approach enables learners to think beyond their immediate responsibilities and to recognize their role within a larger framework. By exposing students to the perspectives of multiple stakeholders, I support the development of a systems oriented mindset that values collaboration and shared responsibility. I encourage learners to identify inefficiencies and gaps within the system and to think critically about how these can be addressed in a coordinated and effective manner. My teaching therefore aims not only to develop individual competence but also to prepare learners to contribute to system level improvement. This holistic perspective ensures that graduates are capable of working efficiently within complex environments and of engaging constructively with colleagues from different roles and disciplines.

Interdisciplinary and Cross‑Domain Expertise

My teaching is strongly informed by an interdisciplinary perspective that reflects the reality of healthcare practice. I recognize that healthcare professionals are educated within their respective disciplines but must work collaboratively to achieve shared goals centered on patient care. This disconnect between education and practice often creates challenges in coordination and communication. In response, I design my teaching to acknowledge the uniqueness of each discipline while actively promoting integration and collaboration. I incorporate perspectives from clinical practice, management, education, and policy to illustrate how different roles contribute to patient outcomes. This approach allows learners to understand both their own responsibilities and the responsibilities of others. By emphasizing common goals and shared accountability, I encourage students to move beyond discipline specific thinking and to adopt a more cooperative mindset. I create learning experiences that simulate real world interactions, where coordination between different roles is essential. This not only enhances understanding but also prepares learners for the practical realities of healthcare environments. My interdisciplinary approach therefore supports the development of professionals who are not only technically competent but also capable of working effectively within diverse and collaborative teams. This alignment between education and practice is essential for achieving high quality care and improving system performance.

Clear Communication and Meaningful Knowledge Translation

Clear communication is a central component of my teaching practice, particularly in contexts where learners come from diverse linguistic and educational backgrounds. I have extensive experience teaching in more than one language and engaging with students who may not share the same first language as the medium of instruction. This has strengthened my ability to present complex concepts in a clear and accessible manner. By prioritizing clarity, I help learners build confidence and develop the ability to engage with complex material.

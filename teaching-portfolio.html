Biography

My teaching is shaped by a comprehensive, learner centered approach that integrates global awareness, practical experience, and interdisciplinary understanding. Drawing on experience across diverse cultural and educational contexts, I create inclusive learning environments that recognize and value the backgrounds and needs of all students. I bring a holistic perspective to healthcare education, enabling learners to understand not only their individual roles but also the broader systems in which they operate. My teaching experience spans a wide range of audiences, from undergraduate students to advanced researchers and community members, allowing me to adapt methods, pace, and content to suit different levels and needs. I emphasize interdisciplinary collaboration, reflecting the realities of healthcare practice, and prepare learners to work effectively across professional boundaries. My teaching is grounded in clinical experience and consistently oriented toward real world application, ensuring that knowledge is meaningful and relevant. As an active researcher, I integrate evidence based principles into my teaching, guiding learners in critical thinking and systematic problem solving. I adopt a flexible and adaptive approach that recognizes individual differences, providing tailored support to help each learner achieve their full potential. Clear communication is central to my practice, particularly in diverse and multilingual contexts, where I ensure that complex concepts are accessible without compromising accuracy. I also have extensive experience supporting learners who face challenges, using structured and empathetic approaches to build confidence and competence. My teaching is further strengthened by the ability to adapt to resource constrained environments, using creative strategies to deliver effective and impactful education under varying conditions.

Teaching Philosophy

I believe that every learner brings unique strengths, and that the role of a teacher is to create the conditions where those strengths can flourish. My philosophy is rooted in respect, evidence, and the conviction that healthcare education must always circle back to the patient and the family.

Mission

To equip current and future healthcare professionals with the knowledge, skills, and compassion they need to deliver evidence‑based, culturally sensitive care — using the best available resources and a deep understanding of each learner’s journey.

Strategy

My teaching strategy combines interactive, case‑based methods with structured mentorship. I adapt content to the learner’s level and context, integrating real‑world clinical experience and the latest research findings. Clear communication and continuous feedback are the pillars of my approach, ensuring that every session is both rigorous and inclusive.

What
I Do
  • Create dynamic, evidence‑based learning experiences.
  • Respect diverse backgrounds and learning styles.
  • Bridge theory with real‑world clinical practice.
  • Adapt teaching to the learner’s level and needs.
  • Use interactive, case‑based and simulation methods.
  • Provide structured mentorship and continuous feedback.
  • Integrate the latest research into every session.
  • Communicate clearly, even across language barriers.
  • Support struggling learners with patience and empathy.
  • Achieve the best outcomes with available resources.
What
I Avoid
  • Monologue lectures that leave no room for questions.
  • Ignoring the unique cultural context of each learner.
  • Teaching theory without clinical application.
  • Assuming all students learn at the same pace.
  • Relying on outdated materials and methods.
  • Withholding constructive, growth‑focused feedback.
  • Presenting opinions as facts without evidence.
  • Using jargon that confuses rather than clarifies.
  • Giving up on learners who struggle.
  • Blaming limited resources for poor outcomes.

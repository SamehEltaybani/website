Biography

With a PhD in Health Science and Nursing, my teaching approach is learner-centered and combines academic rigor, practical relevance, and global perspectives. I teach critical care and emergency nursing, research ethics and integrity, research methodology, scientific writing, and interdisciplinary courses. My teaching spans both face-to-face and online settings, and I have had the privilege of teaching students from more than 50 countries. I aim to prepare students for academic success but the complexities of contemporary healthcare practice and research.

  • Teaching undergraduate, postgraduate, and post-doctoral research fellows.
  • Integrating clinical experience and active research into teaching.
  • Fostering critical thinking, interdisciplinary collaboration, and ethical decision-making.
  • Supporting learners with diverse backgrounds, learning needs, and resource contexts.
  • Clear, engaging, and inclusive communication.

Teaching Philosophy

Every learner brings unique strengths, and the teacher's role is to create the conditions where those strengths can flourish. My philosophy is rooted in respect, evidence, and the conviction that healthcare education must always circle back to the patient and the family.

Mission

To equip healthcare professionals with the knowledge, skills, and compassion they need to deliver evidence‑based, culturally sensitive care using the best available resources and a deep understanding of each learner’s uniqeness.

Strategy

I combine interactive, case‑based methods with structured mentorship. I adapt content to the learner’s level and context, integrating real‑world clinical experience and the latest research findings. Clear communication and continuous feedback.

Core Values in Teaching

Respect

Every student’s background and pace are honoured.

Equity

Accessible education for all, regardless of starting point.

Innovation

Continuously evolving methods for modern healthcare education.

Compassion

Teaching that cares for the learner and the patients they serve.

What
I Do
  • Create dynamic, evidence‑based learning experiences.
  • Respect diverse backgrounds and learning styles.
  • Bridge theory with real‑world clinical practice.
  • Adapt teaching to the learner’s level and needs.
  • Use interactive, case‑based and simulation methods.
  • Provide structured mentorship and continuous feedback.
  • Integrate the latest research into every session.
  • Communicate clearly, even across language barriers.
  • Support struggling learners with patience and empathy.
  • Achieve the best outcomes with available resources.
What
I Avoid
  • Monologue lectures that leave no room for questions.
  • Ignoring the unique cultural context of each learner.
  • Teaching theory without clinical application.
  • Assuming all students learn at the same pace.
  • Relying on outdated materials and methods.
  • Withholding constructive, growth‑focused feedback.
  • Presenting opinions as facts without evidence.
  • Using jargon that confuses rather than clarifies.
  • Giving up on learners who struggle.
  • Blaming limited resources for poor outcomes.

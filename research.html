My Work

I conduct healthcare research that improves the quality of care for vulnerable populations and their families, with particular interests in older adults, critically ill patients, and healthcare education.

I contribute throughout the complete research lifecycle—from developing research questions and securing funding to implementation, analysis, publication, peer review, and post-publication engagement.

I investigate research integrity from a broad systems perspective, examining how responsible research practices strengthen the quality, transparency, and trustworthiness of scientific evidence.

I collaborate with multidisciplinary and international partners to generate evidence that addresses both local healthcare priorities and global challenges.

My Motive

I believe that research should create meaningful improvements in healthcare by informing clinical practice, education, management, and policy rather than existing solely as academic output.

I am driven by a commitment to producing evidence that is methodologically rigorous, ethically conducted, and relevant to the needs of patients, healthcare professionals, and society.

I value international collaboration because diverse perspectives strengthen scientific inquiry and enable more innovative, inclusive, and impactful research.

I am committed to advancing a research culture built on integrity, transparency, continuous learning, and shared responsibility across the scientific community.

My Approach

I view research as an interconnected process in which each stage—from conceptualization to post-publication engagement—contributes to the credibility, quality, and impact of the final outcome.

I integrate qualitative, quantitative, and mixed-methods approaches, selecting methodologies that best address complex healthcare questions while maintaining methodological rigor.

I combine scientific evidence with clinical insight and stakeholder engagement to ensure that research remains both academically robust and practically applicable.

I continually refine my work through interdisciplinary collaboration, critical reflection, and a systems-thinking perspective that promotes innovation, reproducibility, and long-term impact.

What I Do

I design and lead rigorous research studies that address critical questions in gerontological nursing and long‑term care quality.

Why I Do It

Every study I undertake aims to improve the lives of older adults and support the clinicians who care for them.

How I Work

I combine robust quantitative methods with deep clinical insight, always grounding findings in real‑world practice.

Value‑Based Research

Every study I undertake is driven by core principles that ensure rigour, relevance, and real‑world impact.

1

Integrity

Research conducted with honesty and transparency.

Every project follows strict ethical guidelines. Data is never manipulated, and findings are reported truthfully — even when they are not favourable.

2

Rigour

Sound methodology and meticulous analysis.

Every study is designed with robust sampling, validated instruments, and careful statistical control so that conclusions are trustworthy and reproducible.

3

Relevance

Addressing questions that matter to clinicians and patients.

I prioritise research that fills real knowledge gaps and has the potential to change practice, policy, or patient outcomes in measurable ways.

4

Collaboration

Great research is a team effort.

I actively build international partnerships with clinicians, academics, and policymakers to bring diverse perspectives and accelerate translation into practice.

5

Impact

Research that changes practice and policy.

The ultimate measure of success is whether findings lead to better healthcare. Every project is designed with a clear pathway to real‑world implementation.

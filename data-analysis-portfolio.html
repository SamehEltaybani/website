Biography

My work in data analysis is characterized by a strong integration of statistical rigor, clinical understanding, and practical relevance. I approach each dataset with a solid foundation in statistics, ensuring that analytical methods are carefully selected and applied to produce valid, robust, and meaningful conclusions. My background in health sciences and nursing allows me to interpret data within its full real world context, recognizing that each data point reflects underlying clinical, organizational, and human factors. This perspective enables me to generate insights that are not only technically accurate but also aligned with the needs and priorities of stakeholders across healthcare systems. I bring extensive experience in both quantitative and qualitative analysis, allowing me to capture the full complexity of healthcare data and to integrate multiple forms of evidence when needed. My work is informed by continuous engagement with diverse stakeholders, ensuring that outputs are relevant, actionable, and responsive to real world challenges. I maintain a strong commitment to adaptability and ongoing learning, integrating new analytical methods and tools while preserving methodological integrity. My expertise in data storytelling ensures that findings are communicated clearly through well designed visualizations and structured outputs that facilitate understanding and decision making. I am proficient in a wide range of analytical tools and platforms, selecting and combining them as appropriate to meet project needs. My work is grounded in strict adherence to data governance and ethical standards, reflecting a commitment to confidentiality and responsible use of information. Supported by continuous professional development and a transparent portfolio, I deliver data analysis that is rigorous, insightful, and closely connected to practice.

My Approach

“I don’t let the tools drive the analysis. I start from the clinical question, choose the right method, and let the data speak clearly — so that the moment you see my output, you already have the answer.”

What
I Do
  • Choose the most appropriate statistics for each dataset.
  • Interpret data within the full healthcare context.
  • Work equally well with qualitative and quantitative data.
  • Engage diverse stakeholders to shape meaningful questions.
  • Continuously learn and apply the best available techniques.
  • Design visuals and dashboards that speak for themselves.
  • Choose the best tool for each task, integrating when needed.
  • Follow the highest ethical and data‑governance standards.
  • Maintain recognised professional certifications.
  • Showcase my work transparently in a living portfolio.
What
I Avoid
  • Applying statistics without checking assumptions.
  • Ignoring the clinical story behind the numbers.
  • Relying only on quantitative data when qualitative data is available.
  • Working in isolation without stakeholder input.
  • Using outdated methods when better ones exist.
  • Creating fancy graphics that hide the true message.
  • Sticking to one tool regardless of the task.
  • Taking shortcuts with sensitive or confidential data.
  • Equating certificates with competence.
  • Hiding my methods or results behind closed doors.

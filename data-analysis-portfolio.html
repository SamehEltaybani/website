My Approach

“I don’t let the tools drive the analysis. I start from the clinical question, choose the right method, and let the data speak clearly — so that the moment you see my output, you already have the answer.”

What I Do
  • Choose the most appropriate statistics for each dataset.
  • Interpret data within the full healthcare context.
  • Work equally well with qualitative and quantitative data.
  • Engage diverse stakeholders to shape meaningful questions.
  • Continuously learn and apply the best available techniques.
  • Design visuals and dashboards that speak for themselves.
  • Choose the best tool for each task, integrating when needed.
  • Follow the highest ethical and data‑governance standards.
  • Maintain recognised professional certifications.
  • Showcase my work transparently in a living portfolio.
What I Avoid
  • Applying statistics without checking assumptions.
  • Ignoring the clinical story behind the numbers.
  • Relying only on quantitative data when qualitative data is available.
  • Working in isolation without stakeholder input.
  • Using outdated methods when better ones exist.
  • Creating fancy graphics that hide the true message.
  • Sticking to one tool regardless of the task.
  • Taking shortcuts with sensitive or confidential data.
  • Equating certificates with competence.
  • Hiding my methods or results behind closed doors.
